The Proposal: -
About 85 per cent of the state's drinking water resources are groundwater-based. The groundwater table is depleting due to the state's geologic structure and recent rainfall losses at the regional level as well as for irrigation and industrial purposes. This results in the distribution of the water supply. Therefore, the government was considering strengthening of groundwater-based water supply sources through various recharge measures to maintain continuity in groundwater-based source water supply. Based on this, the following decisions are being taken:
The Government's Decisions:
The Government is approving the provision of 2% of funds from the funds allowable for regular schemes under the District Annual Plan (General) with effect from the year 2026-27 for 'implementation of groundwater monitoring and development system ground water measures for resource sustainability of AMAL based tap water supply schemes and raising the total groundwater table by large scale recharge of ground water at field level'.
2. The Department of Water Supply and Sanitation shall take immediate action to issue guidelines and take audits with the approval of the competent authorities for appropriate utilization of 2% of the funds to be earmarked under the District Annual Plan (General) for "Implementation of Ground Water Surveying and Development System Ground Water Measures to sustain the sources of groundwater-based tap water supply schemes and to increase the total ground water table by large-scale recharge of ground water at field level."
